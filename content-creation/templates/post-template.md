# LinkedIn Post Template

Use this template to structure your posts quickly.

---

## Quick Post Template (Handwritten Style)

**Best for:** Quick observations, insights (50-100 words)

```
[Start with context or observation]

[Develop the thought naturally]

[Let it end where it ends - no forced conclusion]

[Optional: Hashtags]
```

**Example 1 (Startups/Product):**
```
Most founders skip validation because it feels slower than building.

Then they spend 6 months building something nobody wants.

Validation isn't slow. Building the wrong thing is.

#Startups #ProductManagement #Validation
```

**Example 2 (Marketing):**
```
Everyone wants to go viral.

Nobody wants to do the boring work of showing up consistently for 2 years.

Viral is luck. Consistency is strategy.

#Marketing #ContentStrategy #SocialMedia
```

**Example 3 (Leadership):**
```
Your team doesn't need another motivation speech.

They need you to remove the blockers you created.

Leadership is less inspiring, more de-risking.

#Leadership #Management #TeamCulture
```

## Structured Post Template

**Best for:** Educational content, frameworks (100-200 words)

```
[HOOK - 1-2 lines that stop the scroll]

[BODY - Personal insight or educational content]
[Use short sentences. One idea per line.]
[Include specific examples, numbers, or stories]

[CONCLUSION - Actionable advice or clear POV]

[HASHTAGS - Max 5]
```

**Example 1 (Product):**
```
I used to think product management was about features.

Then I joined a venture builder. We killed 98% of ideas before building them.

That's when I learned: The best PMs aren't feature factories. They're hypothesis killers.

They validate before they build. They talk to 50 people before writing code. They kill bad ideas in days, not months.

Speed kills bad ideas. Slow kills good ones.

#ProductManagement #VentureBuilding #Validation #Startups
```

**Example 2 (Sales):**
```
I used to think sales was about closing.

Then I tracked my pipeline for 6 months. 80% of lost deals were lost in discovery, not closing.

That's when I learned: The best salespeople aren't closers. They're qualifiers.

They ask hard questions upfront. They walk away from bad fits. They close deals in discovery, not at the end.

Qualify hard, close easy.

#Sales #B2BSales #SalesStrategy #Pipeline
```

## Story Template

**Best for:** Personal anecdotes with lessons

```
[SETTING THE SCENE]

[WHAT HAPPENED - The story]

[WHAT I LEARNED]

[HOW YOU CAN APPLY IT]

#Hashtags
```

```
Didn't talk to users. Didn't validate. Just built what I thought was cool.

Launch day: Crickets.

That feature is still at 0.2% adoption. But it taught me more than any successful launch:

Validation isn't optional. It's the whole game.

Talk to 50 people before you write a line of code.

If they don't say "I need this," you just saved yourself 3 months.

#ProductManagement #StartupLessons #Validation
```

## Choosing Your Template

**Use Handwritten** when:
- You just have a quick thought
- Under 100 words
- Personal observation
- Want it to feel spontaneous

**Use Structured** when:
- Educational deep dive
- Over 100 words
- Teaching a framework
- Want maximum clarity

**Use Specific Framework** when:
- You have a story (Story Template)
- You learned a lesson (Before/After)
- You're giving advice (Problem/Solution)
- Breaking down a topic (List Template)

---

## Post-Writing Checklist

Before you hit post:

- [ ] **Hook works?** First 2 lines stop the scroll?
- [ ] **Value delivered?** Reader learns something concrete?
- [ ] **Sounds like you?** Not generic/AI voice?
- [ ] **Numbers included?** Specific > vague
- [ ] **Hashtags relevant?** Max 5, mix broad + niche
- [ ] **Read aloud?** Would you say this to a friend?
- [ ] **Humanizer applied?** See humanizer-protocol.md

---

## Length Guidelines

**Short (50-100 words):**
- Quick observations
- Single insights
- Contrarian takes
- Takes 5-15 mins to write

**Medium (100-200 words):**
- Educational posts
- Frameworks
- Case studies
- Takes 30-60 mins to write

**Long (200+ words):**
- Deep dives
- Multiple frameworks
- Long stories
- Takes 60-90 mins to write

**Recommendation:** Start with short. Master that first.

---

## Posting Schedule

**Consistency > Frequency**

- 2-3x per week is enough
- Best days: Tuesday-Thursday
- Best times: 7-9 AM, 12-1 PM, 5-6 PM (your timezone)
- Anchor post mid-week (high-value content)
- Flex posts other days (lighter observations)

---

## After You Post

**Engagement is key:**

- Reply to comments within first 2 hours (boosts reach)
- Ask questions in replies to keep conversation going
- Like and thank everyone who engages
- Don't ghost your own post
